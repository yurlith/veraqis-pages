Prices are hypotheses; checkout is not live. The tiers below are the planned commercial model for VERAQIS Recovery. During the current public beta there is no charge and no license file is required. Analysis is never degraded to push a purchase.

What the code actually enforces

The CLI and desktop app consume one entitlement policy, kept separate from the recovery engine. The intended boundary is simple — analysis and preview are free; saving the recovered output is paid. The current public-beta override grants every operation without a license while we gather feedback.

  • Analysis, diagnosis, verification, reports, safe inspection and extraction are free.
  • Structural and Tier-3 semantic recovery, salvage, reconcile and streaming are free.
  • Single-file and folder-level Shield protection, checking and repair are free.
  • Exact-copy reconstruction remains experimental and hidden by default, but enabling it does not require a license during beta.
  • No telemetry. Future licensing is designed for offline signed files, with no network or call-home.

Recovery is always best-effort and is never guaranteed. See the Terms of Use.

Early-access questions

The current commercial boundary of the public beta.

What is free?

Full damaged-ZIP analysis and preview are always free and never degraded. Saving the recovered output is the paid step (Personal / Technician). The Android Archiver is free too. During the current public beta there is no charge at all.

Why is analysis free?

So you can see exactly what is verified, damaged or unknown before you decide to pay. We sell proof of what is recoverable, not a blind promise.

Will licensing be added later?

It is being implemented as offline infrastructure. No beta operation requires it today; future pricing and activation will be communicated before the policy changes.
